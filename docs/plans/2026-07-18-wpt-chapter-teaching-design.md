# WPT chapter teaching design

## Goal

Revise the WPT chapter into a standalone educational introduction that also
explains why each transform property matters to the project's experiment
tracks.

## Narrative

The chapter will proceed from coordinate changes and multiscale locality, to
DWT and WPT trees, to a small Haar example, to best-basis selection, then to
the choice of an axis in LLM tensors. Comparisons to Fourier, STFT/Gabor, and
KLT will be framed by experimental consequences rather than by a catalogue of
definitions.

The project connection will become an explicit experiment map. It will separate
feature-space checkpoint compilation, channel-neighborhood discovery, and
sequence-axis/block-causal generation by transformed axis, target hypothesis,
required controls, and what success would not establish.

## Teaching boundaries

The chapter will distinguish an orthogonal coordinate change from sparsity,
block-diagonality, quality preservation, kernel speed, and continual-learning
benefit. These are measurements or approximations to test, not consequences of
applying WPT.
